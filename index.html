Event Details

About Girls Do Hack

Girls Do Hack is a one-day event designed to empower young women to consider STEM careers. On November 14, 2015, teams of girls will come to the Adler Planetarium to work with women in science, technology, engineering and math careers. Together they will explore the skills required to pursue and excel in a STEM career through a series of hands-on workshops.

On the Day

Each female STEM professional mentor will be paired with two youth participants. Together each team will participate in workshops designed to highlight skills required in STEM careers. These workshops and activities will be developed by Adler Planetarium staff and partner organizations to reflect a cross-section of STEM careers, and may include developing mobile phone apps, prototyping and designing buildings, exoplanet detection, building robots and game design.

Mentors and youth participants may register to participate in one of two cohorts. Each cohort will consist of approximately 40 girls and 20 mentors.

Workshop Descriptions

STEM workshops at Girls Do Hack are designed to highlight the skills necessary to pursue and excel in a STEM career. In these workshops, designed by Adler Planetarium staff and community partner organizations, young women and their STEM mentors will discover and learn together!

Getting to the Adler

Girls Do Hack is easily accessible by car, public transport, bike or foot! For more details please visit the 'Getting There' page on the Adler Planetarium website.
